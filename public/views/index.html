This is a study by the Max Planck Institute for Research on Collective Goods in Germany. The goal is to understand how people make decisions.

Can I take part?

Anyone who can complete the study while not wearing glasses can take part. The study will be conducted in English, and you can participate if you understand English well. You need a desktop computer with a webcam to participate.

What can I expect if I take part in this research study?

This study will take approximately 32 minutes to complete. Your payout consists of a fixed amount, 3.75£, and a variable bonus payment that depends on your decisions in this study and on chance. The total payment will be about 5.50£ on average.

General Information

You will be asked about personal attitudes and demographic information. However, we do not ask for your name or sensitive biographic information. All information that you provide is completely anonymous. Your records will be randomly assigned a subject code number by the system.

Eye Tracking

This study uses your webcam for eye tracking. The data that we will be recording is where you appear to be looking at your computer screen. There will be NO video of you sent to us. The data consists of only two numbers, indicating the position of your gaze on the screen. We do NOT record your screen. We do NOT record your actual video footage.

To participate you must allow the experiment to use your camera on the next screen. If you do not want the experiment to use your camera, please close the page.

What should I know about a research study?

  • If you take part is up to you.
  • To our knowledge, there are no risks associated with taking part in this study.
  • You can stop participating at any time.
  • Your decisions will not be held against you.
  • There are no negative consequences for you if you decide to stop participating.
  • To secure the transparency of scientific findings, the completely anonymized data set will be published and made available to other researchers. Please keep in mind that the potential purposes of future analyses of the data cannot be determined.

Who can I talk to?

If you have questions, concerns, complaints, or think the research has hurt you, please contact Rima-Maria Rahal via rahal@coll.mpg.de.
